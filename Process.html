Our Process

Crafting The Perfect Ride

Surfing is more than just a sport; it's a way of life for many. At the heart of this thrilling pursuit lies a carefully crafted piece of equipment - the surfboard. Each surfboard is a masterpiece of design, engineering, and artistry

Materials Selection

The first crucial step in crafting a surfboard is selecting the right materials. Traditionally, surfboards are made from foam and fiberglass. Polyurethane foam blanks are commonly used due to their lightweight and buoyant properties. More recently, epoxy foam blanks have gained popularity for their durability. The outer shell, or skin, is typically made of fiberglass cloth and epoxy resin.

Shaping The Blank

Once the materials are chosen, the next step is shaping the foam blank into the desired surfboard outline. This is where the surfboard shaper's artistry comes into play. Shapers use templates, saws, and sanding tools to create the board's distinctive curves and contours. The goal is to achieve the right rocker (the curve from nose to tail) and rail shape to optimize performance.

Glassing The Board

After the blank is shaped, it's time to add the fiberglass and epoxy resin. We apply multiple Layers of fiberglass cloth to the foam core, and epoxy resin is brushed and squeegeed onto the cloth. This process is repeated multiple times to build up the board's strength and rigidity. The number of layers and the arrangement of the cloth can be customized to achieve specific characteristics, such as flex and strength.

Laminating And Sanding

Once the fiberglass and resin have cured, the board is laminated, which involves sanding the surface to achieve a smooth and polished finish. This step is essential for reducing drag and ensuring the board moves efficiently through the water. It also prepares the surface for artwork or graphics.

Artwork And Graphics

Many surfboards feature eye-catching artwork and graphics. These designs can be applied using airbrushing, decals, or hand-painting techniques. The art not only enhances the board's aesthetics but also reflects the surfer's personality.

Fins And Leash Plugs

To complete the surfboard, fin boxes and leash plugs are installed. The type and arrangement of fins significantly impact a board's stability and maneuverability. Some surfers prefer single fins, while others opt for multiple fins or "thruster" setups. Leash plugs provide a secure attachment point for the surfer's leash.

Final Quality Check

Before a surfboard is ready to hit the waves, it undergoes a thorough quality check. Shapers and craftsmen inspect the board for any imperfections, ensuring it meets the our specifications and performance standards.

Test Ride And Adjustments

For custom surfboards, the final step often involves a test ride by the surfer. This provides an opportunity to assess the board's performance and make any necessary adjustments to the design, such as fin placement or rocker.

Crafting a surfboard is a meticulous and artistic process that combines science, skill, and passion. Each surfboard tells a unique story, from the selection of materials to the final ride on the waves. Whether you're a professional surfer or a casual enthusiast, we understanding the intricate process of surfboard making. And we have a deeper appreciation for the sport and our team will provide incredible craftsmanship behind every ride.
